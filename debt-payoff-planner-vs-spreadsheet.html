What spreadsheets do well

Spreadsheets are flexible. They can list balances, due dates, minimum payments, interest rates, and notes. They are a reasonable starting point for users who enjoy building their own payoff table and manually maintaining formulas.

The hard part is maintenance. Balances change, payment dates move, formulas break, and progress becomes scattered across tabs. A spreadsheet also does not naturally guide a user through a repayment workflow or keep payoff progress visible in an app-first experience.
